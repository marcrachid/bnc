KEY BIODIVERSITY AREA

This area is known for Biodiversity richness which is endemic to Madagascar.

  • The site is habitat of the Fruits Bats, Madagascar flying fox which is classified as Vulnerable in IUCN Red List. The existence of fruit bats is ecologically significant as they act as a major component in seed dispersal, hence help in the reforestation.
  • There is a large troops of crowned lemurs (Eulemur coronatus )which is classiﬁed as Endangered (EN) in the IUCN Red List. They are an essential part of functioning ecosystem, as they are directly related to the structure and composition of habitats by their role as seed dispersers (Brewer and Rejmanek,1999), pollinators (Jansen et al.1981; Fleming and Sosa,1994;Carthew and Goldingay,1995)
  • The project site has an important 2km of Mangrove forest. The main species of mangrove trees there are Avicennia marina), Rhizophora mucronata), Sonneratia alba), Bruguiera gymnorrhiza), and Ceriops tagal). The Mangrove forest is also where the habitants of Ampombofofo collect crabs and honey.
  • The site is habitat of the Big Baobab which is endemic to Madagascar, such as Andasonia Madagascarensis. For the local communities Baobabs are considered as sacred tree
  • There are 63 bird species, where some of them are classiﬁed as least Concern in IUCN red list and some are classified as) and among these 63 species, 32 species are either endemic to Madagascar or endemic to Indian Ocean region.
  • The site is habitat of 31 butterfly species including the (Pharmacofagus Antennor) which is referred to as indication of healthy environment which is rich in medicinal plants
  • The site is habitat of 10 amphibian species , 39 reptile species including the big chameleon fulcifer pardalis and the snake Langaha Madagascarensis, Brookesia stumpffi which is classiﬁed as Vulnerable in IUCN Red list and one of the project beach is a place where the Green sea turtles (Chelonia mydas) lay eggs which classiﬁed as Endangered on the IUCN Red list

ACTIVITIES AND OFFERS

RESEARCH AND DOCUMENTATION

The Ampombofofo site has at least 63 species of birds and a large group of Eulemur Coronatus. There will also be a possibility of visit (Birds watch and Lemurs watch). You can carry out research and studies in anthropology, primatology, ornithology and botany.
